Coffee Consumption

Overview

Coffee is a popular beverage drank around the world. It's primarily known to be used for boosting energy, but there are many alternative usages too. It can be used for socialization, health benefits, and taste! Coffee is the second most popular drink in the world, just behind water. However, 66% of Americans consume coffee daily which is even more than the percentage of bottled water that falls around 64%. Each year, approximately 400 billion cups are consumed. This is ~5.5 billion pounds of coffee beans! Just in America, about 400 million cups of coffee are consumed on the daily, which averages around 3 cups per person. 2 out of 3 Americans brew coffee at home every day! Within the past year coffee prices have risen around 20%!

Health Benefits

In moderation, coffee offers many health benefits, and studies have linked it to reducing the chances of several health concerns!

Caffine can be beneficial for improving moods, alertness, decreasing fatigue. This is done by blocking adenosine recpetors in the brain; adenosine releases signals to slow down neural activity, promoting tiredness. In turn, dopamine, which inecreases pleasure and motivation, is released easier and stays in the synapse longer. Studies have also linked moderate consumption to lowering risks of depression and anxiety.

Some studies have shown that drinking 3-4 cups of coffee per day can lower the risk of stroke, type 2 diabetes, heart disease, cardiovascular disease, and cancer in the mouth, throat, and digestive system. Additionally, studies have demonstrated the link between coffee and lower risk of neurodegenerative diseases such as, Parkinson's, dementia, and Alzhiemer's in some groups.

Coffee contains many essential nutrients such as potassium, magnesium, riboflavin, and niacin. It is also protective for the liver because it liver enzyme function. Coffee also releases a hormone, gastrin, which increases colon activity, regulating bowel movements. There are many different ways to prepare coffee, making it a delicious drink in the morning!

Coffee Types

Latte

Steamed milk to espresso ratio of about 2:1 or 3:1

Espresso

Weight of ground coffee to the weight of liquid espresso ratio about 1:2

Black

Coffee to water by weight ratio is typically around 1:15 to 1:18

Mocha

Espresso to milk and chocolate ratio of about 1:3

Americano

Espresso to hot water ratio of about 1:2 or 1:3

Macchiato

Espresso to milk foam ratio of about 2:1

Cappuccino

Espresso to steamed milk to foam ratio of about 1:1:1

Cold Brew
